How to Identify Genuine Fertilizers

Learn essential steps to verify fertilizer quality and avoid fraudulent products

Step 1

Check the Packaging

Proper packaging is the first indicator of genuine fertilizer quality.

What to Verify:

  • Manufacturer's name and complete address clearly printed
  • ISI/FCO (Fertilizer Control Order) certification mark
  • Manufacturing date and expiry/best before date
  • Batch number and lot number for traceability
  • Net weight mentioned in kg
  • MRP (Maximum Retail Price) printed
  • Sealed bag without any tampering or damage
  • Nutrient composition (N:P:K ratio) clearly stated

‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags:

Poor printing quality, missing labels, damaged seals, or unclear manufacturer details indicate potential fake products.

Step 2

Conduct Physical Tests

Simple tests you can perform at home or in the field to check fertilizer quality.

Physical Inspection Tests:

  • Color Test: Urea should be white, DAP should be brown/black granules, SSP should be grey
  • Texture Test: Granules should be free-flowing, uniform size, not lumpy or sticky
  • Smell Test: Urea has mild ammonia smell (strong smell indicates adulteration)
  • Water Solubility Test: Genuine fertilizer dissolves completely in water within 5-10 minutes
  • Touch Test: Should not leave excessive residue or sticky coating on hands
  • Visual Inspection: No foreign particles, stones, or unusual materials mixed in

üí° Pro Tip:

Always test a small sample before using bulk fertilizer. Dissolve in water - genuine fertilizer dissolves completely, fake may leave residue.

Step 3

Verify Authenticity

Use modern verification methods to confirm fertilizer authenticity.

Verification Methods:

  • QR Code: Scan the QR code on packaging to verify product details online
  • Batch Number: Cross-check batch number with manufacturer's website or helpline
  • Authorized Dealer: Always buy from government-authorized dealers or cooperatives
  • Purchase Receipt: Keep bill/receipt for quality complaints and warranty claims
  • Online Verification: Use state agriculture department apps for product verification
  • Lab Testing: For bulk purchases, get sample tested at soil testing lab

‚úÖ Genuine Dealer Signs:

Licensed dealers display authorization certificate, maintain proper records, and provide original bills with all details.

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs of Fake Fertilizers

Be alert for these red flags when purchasing fertilizers

Suspicious Pricing

Price significantly lower than market rate or authorized dealer price

Poor Packaging

Low quality printing, faded labels, or missing product information

Missing Certifications

No ISI/FCO mark, unclear manufacturer details, or absent batch numbers

Physical Defects

Lumpy texture, unusual color variations, strong chemical odor, or foreign particles

Unauthorized Sellers

Sold by unregistered dealers, roadside vendors, or non-authorized shops

No Proper Documentation

Seller refuses to provide proper bill, receipt, or product warranty

Common Fertilizer Types & Characteristics

Urea (46% N)

Color: Pure white granules or prills

Smell: Mild ammonia odor

Solubility: Completely dissolves in water

Use: Nitrogen source for all crops

DAP (18% N, 46% P)

Color: Brown to dark grey granules

Smell: Mild ammonia smell

Solubility: Dissolves in water

Use: Phosphorus and nitrogen source, good starter fertilizer
